Why this origin

Four reasons to source
from Misiones.

01

Eighty years of Misiones terroir

Plantations seeded in 1943–1945 in Misiones' red iron soil. The subtropical climate, long oxidation and traditional Asian-style firing produce an amber cup with almond, cocoa and ripe-apple notes.

80-year plantations · red iron soil · traditional process

02

Hand-selected, bud by bud

Every harvest follows the same protocol — apical bud, developing leaf, expanded leaf — hand-picked and inspected. Cycles of 7 to 28 days, adjusted to the season.

1 bud + 2 leaves · 100% manual selection · season-adjusted cycles

03

Single plantation, single batch

Full chain of custody from leaf to copper foil. Every shipment carries its own origin dossier.

Single-farm source · single-batch traceability · origin dossier per shipment

04

Shelf differentiation

A fresh origin alongside the great classics of the category.

First hand-pressed tea produced outside China · unique Argentine provenance
